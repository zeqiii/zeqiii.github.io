Flash Nexus Device with Compiled AOSP

10 Jul, 2016 · by zeqi · Read in about 2 min · (215 Words)
Tips

Compiling AOSP

About how to download and build AOSP source code, the android official site has provided us a very detailed instruction, see here

Downloading drivers

What we want is to flash the builded AOSP to a real nexus device, not just test the image on an emulator, so we need to download the drivers for a nexus deivce. From https://developers.google.com/android/nexus/drivers we download sh files to our android source directory.

And here is my baidu cloud share link, driver for Nexus6-android6.0.1 can be downloaded from here too. http://pan.baidu.com/s/1o7LuWvo

Run these sh files and type «I ACCEPT», then a «vender» directory will be created in our android source directory.

Flashing

After the building is completed, we copy these «img» file to some directory.

SDK provide us platforms which contains «fastboot». Connect your device and your PC using an USB cable, shutdown the device then press power button and volume down button at the same time, holding them until the device enters fastboot mode. Then use fastboot devices to see if the devices has connected.

Using fastboot flash command to flash the image into the device. For example.

fastboot flash system E:\system.img
fastboot flash userdata E:\userdata.img
fastboot flash boot E:\boot.img
fastboot flash recovery E:\recovery.img
fastboot flash cache E:\cache.img
fastboot flash XXX YOUR_IMG_FILE

Reboot your device.

fastboot reboot
